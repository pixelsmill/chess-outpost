What is an Outpost?

In chess, an outpost is a piece (typically a knight) placed on an advanced square where it cannot be attacked by enemy pawns. These pieces control key squares and exert long-term pressure on the opponent's position, making them strategically valuable assets.

Identify Strong and Weak Squares

This application helps you visualize and understand square control in chess positions. Through advanced board visualization, you can identify which squares are well-defended, which are weak points in your opponent's position, and where your pieces can establish powerful outposts to dominate the game.
